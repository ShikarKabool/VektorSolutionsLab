The Human Edge Competency Model

Six Capacities AI
Cannot Replicate

As AI absorbs technical work, competitive advantage migrates to distinctly human capacities. Organisations that develop these systematically will compound their lead. Those that neglect them will find their AI investments brittle and prone to failure at the critical moments.

01

Judgement

Sound decision-making under ambiguity, time pressure, and incomplete information — where AI produces probabilities but humans must decide and account for the consequences.

02

Curiosity

The disciplined habit of questioning outputs, challenging norms, and pursuing the second-order question that AI has not been prompted to ask.

03

Professional Scepticism

The rigorous practice of interrogating AI-generated outputs with domain expertise — not blind trust, and not reflexive rejection. The calibrated challenge.

04

Empathy

Reading the human dimensions of decisions — the unspoken concerns, the social dynamics, the lived experience that no model fully captures but every consequential decision must account for.

05

Stewardship

Acting as a custodian of long-term institutional value — making decisions that a future generation of stakeholders will judge as responsible, proportionate, and just.

06

Domain Knowledge

The deep, experiential expertise that enables a practitioner to know when AI is correct, when it is plausible but wrong, and when it is confidently fabricating.
